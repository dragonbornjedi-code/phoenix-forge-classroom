# Childhood Compass: Developmental Navigation

The **Childhood Compass** is the human-centric interpretation of the system's developmental analysis. It replaces the "Blind Spot Illumination Engine" with a more approachable, supportive interface for parents.

## 1. The Core Philosophy: "Needs More Sunlight"
The Compass does not diagnose or judge. It identifies areas of the childhood that are "Thriving" and areas that "Need More Sunlight" (neglected exposure).

### Visual Metaphor
- **Thriving:** Green/Vibrant. (e.g., "Nature Walk: covered frequently")
- **Steady:** Neutral/Balanced. (e.g., "Numeracy: consistent")
- **Needs Sunlight:** Pale/Quiet. (e.g., "Fine Motor Skills: minimal engagement this month")

## 2. Navigation Signals
The Compass uses the **Living Chronicle** and **Accepted Plans** to generate signals.

### Logic
- **Recency:** When was the last time this domain was covered?
- **Frequency:** How often does this subcategory appear in the last 30 days?
- **Identity Alignment:** Does the coverage match the child's **Identity Threads**?

## 3. Usage in the Daily Flow

### Planning Phase
When the parent creates a new expedition, the Compass gently surfaces suggested **Intent Tiles**.
- *Example:* "It's been a while since we worked on Music. Here are three fun ways to bring some rhythm to our walk today!"

### Acceptance Phase
After reordering tiles, the parent sees a "Compass Preview."
- *Example:* "Today's plan brings extra sunlight to **Logic** and **Nature**!"

## 4. Continuity & Resilience
If life gets chaotic and the board isn't used for a week, the Compass observes the gap without "Resetting."
- **Recalibration:** Upon return, the Compass suggests "Minimum Wins" to gently get back into a balanced rhythm.
- **Parental Mastery:** The system rewards the parent's stewardship with encouraging reflections on their consistency over months and years.
