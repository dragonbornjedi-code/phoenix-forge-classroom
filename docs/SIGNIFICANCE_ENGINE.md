# Significance Engine: Navigating 20 Years of Life

Twenty years of childhood will produce tens of thousands of records. The Significance Engine (part of the PCAS Layer) helps navigate this volume by ranking memories without deleting any data.

## 1. Significance Factors
The engine calculates a `significanceScore` (0.0 to 1.0) based on:
- **Parental Marking:** Explicitly marked as a "Legendary Memory" in the Teacher Edition.
- **Emotional Intensity:** Detected via child mood tags or voice-note tone.
- **Family Participation:** Memories involving multiple family members (Relationship Graph density).
- **Frequency of Recall:** How often the child (or Spark) returns to this memory or artifact.
- **Narrative Weight:** Does this event anchor an "Identity Thread" or a "Chronicle Chapter"?

## 2. Navigation Tiers
- **Legendary (Top 1%):** The "Life-Defining" moments. Always displayed in the Museum's "Hall of Great Stories."
- **Major (Top 10%):** Significant milestones. Primary items for Memory Books.
- **Ordinary:** The beautiful everyday moments. Accessible via the Chronicle search.
- **Background:** High-frequency, low-variance data (e.g., daily check-ins). Used for semantic pattern discovery but not highlighted in the Museum.

## 3. Usage in Experience Shells
- **Spark:** Spark prioritizes "Major" and "Legendary" memories for callbacks.
- **Museum:** The "Memory Shelf" automatically rotates "Ordinary" memories but gives permanent space to "Legendary" artifacts.
- **Legacy Viewer:** The adult self can filter the entire history by significance to see a "Highlights Reel" of their childhood.
