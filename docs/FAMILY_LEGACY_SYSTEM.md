# Family Legacy System: Building World Lore from Reality

The Family Legacy System transforms a family's unique culture into the permanent mythology of Hearthhome. It ensures that the digital world feels like a continuation of the physical home.

## 1. Mythology Components

### Sacred Landmarks
- **Concept:** Specific real-world locations that hold meaning.
- **Example:** "The Creek," "The Big Oak," "Grandma's Garden."
- **World Manifestation:** These appear as permanent, named locations on the **Memory Globe**.

### Legendary Companions
- **Concept:** Family pets or recurring animals.
- **Example:** "Rex the Sentinel" (Family Dog).
- **World Manifestation:** The pet is given a "Citizen Status" in Hearthhome. They might appear as NPCs or their portraits appear in the House.

### Ancient Traditions (The Calendar of Joy)
- **Concept:** Recurring family rituals and holidays.
- **Example:** "Friday Night Pizza," "The Annual Camping Trip," "Sunday Morning Stories."
- **World Manifestation:** Hearthhome skins itself for these events. NPCs might mention the upcoming "Feast of the Golden Crust" on Fridays.

### Family Recipes & Wisdom
- **Concept:** Skills or stories passed down by elders.
- **Example:** "Grandpa's Secret Pie," "Dad's Story of the Shadow Dragon."
- **World Manifestation:** These become **Memory Books** or "Recipe Scrolls" in the House.

## 2. Parent-Led Lore Entry (The Mentor Interface)
The Teacher Edition provides a "Mythology Registry" for parents to define these elements.
- **Name Mapping:** Map "Real Name" to "Lore Name" (e.g., `real: "Our Backyard"` -> `lore: "The Training Grounds of the Forgers"`).
- **Trigger Definition:** Define when Spark should mention a piece of lore (e.g., "Mention Rex when the child is at the Project Table").

## 3. The Shared Legend
The system encourages shared storytelling. If Dad tells a story about a dragon, the Parent can "Register the Legend."
- Spark then incorporates that dragon into his dialogue: "Do you think the Shadow Dragon would like this fort?"
- This creates a feedback loop where the child's real-life stories are validated and reinforced by their digital world.

## 4. Emotional Preservation
By encoding these elements into the **ForgeProfile**, they survive the loss of a pet or the move to a new home.
- **The Sentinel's Statue:** If a pet passes away, their digital manifestation can be transitioned into a "Guardian Spirit" or a memorial statue in the House, preserving the relationship for the child's future self.
- **The Old World Mirror:** Moving to a new house allows the child to "Look back" at the old backyard via the **Time Mirror**.
