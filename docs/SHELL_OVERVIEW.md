# Experience Shell Overview: Layer 3

Experience Shells are the interchangeable frontends of Phoenix Forge. They do not own the childhood memory; they consume the **CMOS** and **PCAS** layers to provide a context-appropriate window into the child's world.

## 1. The Multi-Shell Lifecycle

### Stage 1: The Hearth (Ages 5-7)
- **Shell:** Android APK (2D Cozy Style).
- **Interface:** High-contrast, touch-first, Studio Ghibli-inspired illustrations.
- **Goal:** Establishing the core loops of **Discovery** and **Capture**.

### Stage 2: The Village (Ages 8-10)
- **Shell:** Android Tablet + PC Alpha.
- **Interface:** Mixed 2D/3D. Introduction of more complex interactions (Workshop crafting, Market trading).
- **Goal:** Developing **Agency** and **Skill Mastery**.

### Stage 3: The Kingdom (Ages 11-13)
- **Shell:** Godot RPG (Full 3D).
- **Interface:** Third-person exploration. Immersive rendering of the Museum and Realms.
- **Goal:** Exploring **Identity** and **Community**.

### Stage 4: The Universe (Ages 14-18)
- **Shell:** Cross-Platform Master Suite (PC/VR).
- **Interface:** Self-authored mission editors, complex project management.
- **Goal:** Preparing for **Mentorship** and **Handover**.

## 2. Shared Interface Contract
Every Experience Shell must implement the following:
1. **MemoryEvent Emitter:** Ingesting life moments into CMOS.
2. **Chronicle Portal:** Reading and displaying past episodes and human voices.
3. **Artifact Renderer:** Displaying **Legacy Objects** at the highest supported fidelity.
4. **Spark Interface:** Consuming the Spark dialogue templates for the current maturation tier.

## 3. The 2046 Bridge
Each shell is responsible for mapping CMOS data to its native assets using the **Universal ID** system. A `ObjectID: "blue_feather_01"` found in the Android APK is automatically rendered as a high-poly 3D mesh in the Godot shell.
