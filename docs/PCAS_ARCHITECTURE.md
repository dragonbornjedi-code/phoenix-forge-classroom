# Phoenix Forge: The Living Childhood Operating System

## Core Philosophy: Life is the Adventure
Phoenix Forge is not a game the child plays; it is a world that **remembers their life**. It is a **Persistent Childhood Adventure System (PCAS)** where real-world discoveries, family milestones, and personal growth are transformed into the mythology of a living, evolving world.

## The Architectural Hierarchy
1. **The Core (ForgeProfile):** The permanent, encrypted "Black Box" of childhood identity.
2. **The Chronicler (The Living Chronicle):** The narrative engine that turns events into chapters.
3. **The Mirror (Spark & Wisps):** Entities that reflect and mature with the child's real-life growth.
4. **The Manifestation (Hearthhome):** The physical and digital world that visually represents the ForgeProfile.

## Key Systems

### 1. The Living Chronicle
A semantic, narrative database that captures "Episodic Memories" and groups them into "Ages."
- **Entries:** Not "Completed Mission 01," but "The Day of the Sky Feather."
- **Chapters:** Semantic clusters of related events (e.g., "The Summer of the Secret Fort").

### 2. Family Mythology Engine
Allows parents/mentors to register "Sacred Landmarks" (e.g., a specific park), "Legendary Companions" (family pets), and "Ancient Traditions" (holidays/jokes). These are injected into Spark's dialogue and mission generation.

### 3. Legacy Object System
Every photo or project capture is not just a file; it is an **Artifact**. 
- **Metadata:** Captures time, location, mood, and "Wisp Reaction."
- **Permanence:** Designed with a 10-year migration path, ensuring the "Blue Feather" found at age 5 is a high-resolution 3D asset in the Godot world at age 12.

### 4. Companion Lifecycle (Spark)
Spark is a dynamic AI entity with a **Maturation Logic** tied to the child's age in the ForgeProfile.
- **Cognitive Shift:** From simple, playful language to complex, reflective, and philosophical partnership.

### 5. Life-Driven Quest Generation
Instead of a static list, quests are generated based on:
- **Environment:** Real-time weather, location (GPS), and time of year.
- **Physical Mastery:** Real-world build projects (captured via photo).
- **Social Context:** Family events and rituals.

## The 10-Year Evolution
- **The Cottage (Ages 5-7):** Sensory, safe, localized.
- **The Village (Ages 8-10):** Agency, skills, community roles.
- **The Kingdom (Ages 11-13):** Exploration, complexity, identity formation.
- **The Universe (Ages 14-18):** Mastery, mentorship, philosophical depth.
