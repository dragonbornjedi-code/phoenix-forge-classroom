# Identity Formation Engine: The "Who am I?" System

The Identity Formation Engine (IFE) tracks the long-term evolution of the child's personality, interests, and strengths. It answers the question: "Who did I become?"

## 1. Identity Threads
Instead of assigning fixed traits, the system identifies **Threads**—consistent patterns of behavior and interest supported by real-world evidence.

## 2. The Core Archetypes (Wisp Alignment)
Threads are semantically grouped under the four foundational Wisps:
- **EMBER Threads:** Builder, Engineer, Inventor, Fixer.
- **MOSS Threads:** Naturalist, Observer, Protector, Explorer.
- **RIPPLE Threads:** Storyteller, Empath, Communicator, Helper.
- **VOLT Threads:** Pattern-Finder, Logic-Master, Organizer, Strategist.

## 3. Evidence-Based Discovery
Identity is not "assigned" by a parent or an AI; it is **discovered through the Chronicle**.
- **The Observation Loop:** When a parent approves an Artifact in the Teacher Edition, they can "Tag a Thread."
- **Semantic Reinforcement:** If the PCAS semantic engine notices a high frequency of "Nature" episodes, it proposes a "Naturalist" thread to the parent for approval.

## 4. The Self-Reflection Bridge
As the child matures (Ages 11-18), the IFE presents these threads to the user.
- **Spark:** "I've noticed you've always been someone who looks for how things fit together. We've been tracking this 'Builder' thread since you were 5. Does that still feel like you?"
- **Impact:** This allows the child to take active ownership of their own identity formation, transforming data into self-awareness.

## 5. Age 25 Impact
For the adult looking back, the Identity Formation Engine provides a "Legacy Map." It shows not just what they did, but the consistent virtues and interests that shaped their early life.
