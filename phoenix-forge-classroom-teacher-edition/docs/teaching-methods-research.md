# Teaching Methods Research

Teacher Edition method library for a five-and-a-half-year-old learner.

Use this as the app's adult-facing method catalog. Lesson plans should tag which methods they use, why, and what evidence should be collected.

## Method Stack

### Explicit Teaching
Best for new skills, safety routines, phonics, math procedures, tool use, and multi-step tasks.

Pattern:
- State the tiny goal.
- Model it.
- Do it together.
- Let him try one step.
- Give immediate feedback.
- Practice briefly.
- Return later for review.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `explicit-model`
- `guided-practice`
- `prompt-fade`
- `feedback-now`

### Play-Based Learning
Best for early childhood engagement, language, social skills, imagination, math in context, and joyful repetition.

Pattern:
- Start with a real object, story, build, movement, or pretend mission.
- Let him choose within a clear boundary.
- Adult narrates and extends learning.
- Capture evidence through observation, photo, or quick note.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `play-based`
- `child-choice`
- `adult-extended`
- `hands-on`

### Developmental Progression
Best for reading, numeracy, writing, motor skills, and executive-function growth.

Pattern:
- Identify current level.
- Teach the next reachable step.
- Avoid skipping foundations.
- Reassess with small tasks.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `developmental-progression`
- `readiness-check`
- `next-small-step`

### Universal Design For Learning
Best for reducing friction and supporting neurodivergent tendencies without lowering expectations.

Pattern:
- Offer multiple ways to engage.
- Offer multiple ways to understand.
- Offer multiple ways to respond.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `udl-engagement`
- `udl-representation`
- `udl-expression`

### Retrieval And Spaced Practice
Best for memory, vocabulary, phonics, math facts, routines, safety rules, and emotional scripts.

Pattern:
- Ask him to recall instead of only re-reading/re-watching.
- Practice briefly across days.
- Mix old and new skills.
- Keep it game-like.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `retrieval`
- `spaced-practice`
- `interleaving`

### Concrete-Representational-Abstract
Best for math, systems thinking, maps, sequencing, and symbolic logic.

Pattern:
- Concrete: touch and move real objects.
- Representational: draw, map, or use pictures.
- Abstract: use symbols, numbers, arrows, letters, or code blocks.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `concrete`
- `representational`
- `abstract`
- `cra`

### Inquiry And Project-Based Learning
Best after the first model, for science, engineering, maker work, curiosity, and transfer.

Pattern:
- Ask a question.
- Predict.
- Test.
- Observe.
- Change one variable.
- Build the next version.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `inquiry`
- `project-based`
- `one-variable`
- `prototype-test`

### Task Analysis
Best for routines, chores, tool use, hygiene, cooking, transitions, and independent work.

Pattern:
- Break the task into visible micro-steps.
- Teach one step at a time.
- Mark done.
- Fade prompts.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `task-analysis`
- `visual-steps`
- `finished-box`
- `prompt-fade`

### Visual Supports
Best for transitions, emotional regulation, routine memory, language load, and expectations.

Pattern:
- Show the plan.
- Show now/next.
- Show choices.
- Show done.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `visual-schedule`
- `first-then`
- `choice-board`
- `timer`

### Co-Regulation And Regulation Practice
Best for emotional learning, frustration, transitions, and overload.

Pattern:
- Adult regulates first.
- Reduce words.
- Meet body need.
- Use one familiar support.
- Reflect later when calm.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `co-regulation`
- `body-first`
- `low-language`
- `repair-later`

### Social Narratives And Roleplay
Best for boundaries, conflict, sharing, safety, public behavior, and flexible thinking.

Pattern:
- Tell/show a short story.
- Act it with toys or puppets.
- Practice the phrase.
- Try it in a low-stakes real situation.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `social-narrative`
- `roleplay`
- `puppet-practice`
- `script-practice`

### Errorless Start To Productive Struggle
Best for confidence, anxiety, new tasks, and frustration tolerance.

Pattern:
- Start with a version he can succeed at.
- Add one challenge.
- Stop before overload.
- Celebrate the patch/retry.

Teacher Edition tags:
- `errorless-start`
- `productive-struggle`
- `make-easier`
- `make-harder`

## Method Selection Rules

- New safety/tool/routine skill: explicit teaching + task analysis + visual steps.
- New academic skill: explicit model + concrete materials + guided practice.
- Concept exploration: play-based + inquiry after a model.
- Memory target: retrieval + spaced practice + interleaving.
- Transition friction: visual support + first-then + timer + co-regulation.
- Emotional overload: body-first + co-regulation + low language + reflect later.
- Low motivation: choice + interest hook + short win + immediate feedback.
- High energy: movement-first + heavy work + outdoor/physical version.
- Low energy: low-demand variant + fewer steps + quiet evidence collection.

## Teacher Edition Data Fields

Each lesson should store:
- Primary method.
- Secondary methods.
- Support methods.
- Why this method was chosen.
- What cue triggered the method.
- Whether it worked.
- Independence level.
- Next method to try.

## Research Anchors

- IES/What Works Clearinghouse foundational reading guidance supports explicit teaching of phonological awareness, letter-sound links, decoding, word recognition, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension foundations.
- IES/What Works Clearinghouse early math guidance emphasizes developmental progressions, daily math, progress monitoring, and seeing/describing the world mathematically.
- EEF Early Years Evidence Store emphasizes early communication/language, self-regulation, executive function, and early mathematics approaches.
- CAST UDL Guidelines support multiple means of engagement, representation, and action/expression.
- CASEL SEL gives a practical competency map for self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible decision-making.
- AFIRM and IRIS support practical evidence-based practices such as visual supports, prompting, reinforcement, task analysis, and social narratives.
