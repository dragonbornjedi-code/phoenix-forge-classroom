# Inclusive Learning Supports

Teacher Edition support library for neurodivergent tendencies, uneven development, variable attention, sensory needs, strong interests, language differences, anxiety, rigidity, impulsivity, and transition friction.

This is not a diagnostic guide. Track observable cues, match supports to the moment, and review with qualified professionals when safety, health, or development concerns require it.

Working learner stage: five and a half. At this age, support should be embedded in play, routine, movement, visuals, and co-regulation rather than long verbal explanations.

## Core Principles
- Use Universal Design for Learning: offer multiple ways to engage, understand, and respond.
- Assume lagging skill or unmet need before defiance.
- Make expectations visible.
- Reduce language during overload.
- Teach regulation before expecting independent regulation.
- Use interests as access ramps.
- Keep routines predictable, but practice small changes safely.
- Reinforce effort, communication, repair, and flexibility.
- Prefer short successful repetitions over long battles.

## Support Pattern Library

### Visual Supports
Use for transitions, routines, language load, memory, and uncertainty.

Options:
- Visual schedule.
- First-then board.
- Picture choice board.
- Timer.
- Step strip.
- Finished box.
- Color-coded system temperature.
- Map of the day.

Look for:
- Repeated "what now?"
- Refusal at transitions.
- Forgetting steps.
- Anxiety when plans are unclear.

### Predictable Routine With Flexible Choice
Use when structure helps but control battles are rising.

Options:
- Same start ritual.
- Two acceptable choices.
- Choice within order: "math then build" or "build then math."
- Preview change before it happens.
- Use "plan changed" card.

Look for:
- Rigidity.
- Distress when plans change.
- Repeating questions.
- Getting stuck on one expected outcome.

### Task Analysis
Use when a task is too big or has hidden steps.

Options:
- Break into micro-steps.
- Model first.
- Do together.
- Prompt one step.
- Fade prompts.
- Celebrate step completion.

Look for:
- "I can't."
- Running away from task.
- Random action that does not match goal.
- Meltdown near multi-step work.

### Reinforcement And Motivation
Use to make desired behavior visible and worthwhile.

Options:
- Immediate specific praise.
- Token board.
- Choice of next activity.
- Interest-based mission theme.
- Success jar.
- Build toward a project reward.

Look for:
- Low persistence.
- Avoiding practice.
- Needing frequent feedback.
- More effort when task connects to interest.

### Sensory Regulation
Use when body state blocks learning.

Options:
- Heavy work.
- Movement break.
- Noise reduction.
- Dim light.
- Chew/crunch snack if appropriate.
- Water.
- Weighted lap item.
- Quiet space.
- Fidget with rules.

Look for:
- Covering ears.
- Crashing into things.
- Chewing objects.
- Slumping.
- Constant movement.
- Avoiding touch or mess.

### Communication Supports
Use when words are hard, emotions are high, or language load is too much.

Options:
- Pointing choices.
- Emotion cards.
- Yes/no cards.
- "Help", "break", "again", "stop" cards.
- Adult narration.
- Wait time.
- One-step language.

Look for:
- Screaming instead of asking.
- Repeating phrases without action.
- Shutdown.
- Saying no to every verbal prompt.

### Executive Function Supports
Use for starting, planning, shifting, waiting, and finishing.

Options:
- Start cue.
- Countdown.
- Now/next/later board.
- Checklist.
- Timer.
- Work basket.
- Finish line.
- External memory: photos, notes, icons.

Look for:
- Cannot start without adult.
- Leaves task half done.
- Gets lost between rooms.
- Cannot shift from preferred activity.

### Social And Emotional Supports
Use for conflict, boundaries, empathy, and repair.

Options:
- Social story.
- Puppet rehearsal.
- Roleplay.
- Turn cards.
- Repair script.
- Peace mat.
- Boundary words practice.

Look for:
- Grabbing.
- Invading space.
- Hitting during conflict.
- Not noticing another person's cues.
- Shame after mistake.

## Teacher Decision Tree

When a lesson fails:
1. Check body: hunger, thirst, sleep, bathroom, sensory load.
2. Check task: too hard, too long, unclear, too verbal, not enough movement.
3. Check transition: did he know what was ending and what was next?
4. Check motivation: does he know why this matters or what success looks like?
5. Check support: visual, physical, verbal, sensory, choice, or co-regulation.
6. Patch one variable only.
7. Retry smaller.

## Data To Track
- Cue observed.
- Context: time, food, sleep, noise, transition, task type.
- Support used.
- Response.
- Recovery time.
- Independence level.
- Notes for next attempt.

## Independence Scale
- 0: adult completed.
- 1: hand-over-hand or full physical support.
- 2: direct model and do together.
- 3: verbal/visual prompt.
- 4: gesture or environmental cue.
- 5: independent.

## Student Edition Boundary

Student Edition can show:
- Picture schedule.
- First-then.
- Timer.
- Mission choices.
- Help/break buttons.
- Mood/energy check-in.
- Friendly retry prompts.

Student Edition should not show:
- Adult interpretation of behavior.
- Sensitive notes.
- Diagnostic language.
- Pattern analysis.
- Teacher strategy scoring.
