# Curriculum Taxonomy

Teacher Edition taxonomy for organizing every lesson, activity, metric, and Student Edition mission.

Working learner stage: Ezra is five and a half. Design defaults should target late-pre-K/kindergarten readiness while allowing uneven development by subcategory.

The seven top-tier categories stay stable. Subcategories can grow over time. Each subcategory should eventually have lesson plans, activity variants, assessment cues, support methods, and Student Edition mission translations.

## Taxonomy Rules
- Keep every child-facing activity concrete, short, and playful.
- Default to 5-15 minute lessons, with shorter 2-5 minute versions for low-energy or high-friction moments.
- Build from observation to independence: model, do together, prompt, fade support, transfer.
- Store adult reasoning in Teacher Edition only.
- Tag every lesson by category, subcategory, age/stage, energy level, materials, setting, supports, and evidence type.
- Prefer observable metrics: what he did, how much help he needed, what changed, and what support worked.
- Choose teaching methods from `teaching-methods-research.md`.
- Use `subcategory-method-map.md` to select the first method set and starter lesson direction for each subcategory.

## 1. Cognitive & Academic Foundations

Purpose: Build reading, math, thinking, observation, memory, problem solving, and the ability to learn from tools, people, books, and technology.

Subcategories:
- Early Literacy: print awareness, letter recognition, phonological awareness, decoding, sight words, environmental print.
- Oral Language: vocabulary, storytelling, asking questions, retelling events, describing observations.
- Listening Comprehension: following directions, story recall, detail spotting, sequencing heard information.
- Numeracy: counting, one-to-one correspondence, comparison, more/less, simple addition/subtraction stories.
- Spatial Reasoning: position words, maps, shapes, puzzles, rotations, symmetry, construction.
- Pattern Recognition: visual patterns, sound patterns, movement patterns, code-like sequences.
- Scientific Inquiry: observe, predict, test, compare, record, retest.
- Engineering Thinking: problem, constraint, material choice, prototype, test, improve.
- Computational Thinking: sequence, condition, loop, sort, classify, debug.
- Documentation Literacy: picture manuals, diagrams, labels, signs, icons, simple technical docs.
- Memory And Recall: working memory games, matching, sequencing, rehearsal, recall with cues.
- Attention And Task Persistence: start task, stay with task, ask for help, finish small step.
- Executive Function Foundations: plan, inhibit impulse, shift, organize, monitor progress.
- Historical And Cultural Context: then/now, people/tools, family history, inventions, community stories.
- Abstraction Mapping: object jobs, parts/whole, category, material, mechanism, symbolic representation.
- Media And Information Literacy: fact/opinion, trusted adult, source checking, ad recognition, digital caution.

Metrics:
- Follows a 2-4 step sequence.
- Explains a prediction before testing.
- Sorts by one property, then two.
- Transfers a concept to a new object or setting.
- Uses a symbol, word, number, map, or diagram to solve a problem.

Lesson seeds:
- Read the room labels and match objects to picture cards.
- Build a block bridge, test weight, change one design variable.
- Use arrows to make a toy follow a path.
- Compare an old tool photo to a modern household tool.

## 2. Emotional Intelligence & Self-Regulation

Purpose: Build emotional vocabulary, body awareness, safe regulation routines, resilience, agency, and repair after hard moments.

Subcategories:
- Feeling Identification: happy, sad, mad, scared, frustrated, proud, disappointed, calm.
- Body Signal Awareness: hunger, thirst, tiredness, bathroom, tension, heart rate, noise sensitivity.
- Energy And Arousal: blue/yellow/red, low/high energy, wiggly, sleepy, overloaded.
- Co-Regulation: accepting adult calm, breathing together, movement together, sensory reset.
- Independent Regulation: pause, breathe, ask for help, choose break, use recovery hub.
- Transition Support: warnings, visual schedule, first-then, timer, goodbye rituals.
- Frustration Tolerance: try again, reduce step size, ask for help, accept imperfection.
- Delay And Waiting: waiting games, token saving, turn-taking, timer practice.
- Self-Concept And Agency: "I did it", effort recognition, choice ownership, controllable/uncontrollable.
- Mindfulness And Presence: sound hunts, body scans, breath games, quiet minute.
- Repair And Reflection: what happened, what helped, what to try next.
- Safety During Big Feelings: safe body, safe space, adult support, no teaching during red state.

Metrics:
- Points to or names a feeling/state.
- Uses a support before escalation or with a prompt.
- Recovers faster after frustration.
- Accepts a transition cue.
- Can identify one thing that helped.

Lesson seeds:
- Use a color gauge before and after a hard puzzle.
- Practice the pause button during Red Light/Green Light.
- Build and decorate a Recovery Hub.
- Do an "oops patch" after a tower falls.

## 3. Practical Life Skills

Purpose: Build autonomy, body maintenance, household contribution, money sense, tool safety, digital safety, and daily living competence.

Subcategories:
- Hygiene: teeth, hands, bathing, hair, clothes, bathroom routines.
- Sleep And Rest: bedtime routine, quiet time, body battery, wake anchors.
- Nutrition And Hydration: food groups, protein/fiber/water, snack planning, hunger signals.
- Clothing And Weather Readiness: choose clothes, shoes, jacket, rain/sun prep.
- Household Care: tidy, sort, wipe, sweep, laundry basics, dishes, room audit.
- Cooking And Food Prep: wash produce, measure, stir, spread, simple safe prep.
- Tool Safety: ask first, carry safely, wear protection, return tools, adult-only tools.
- Repair And Maintenance: tape, glue, tighten, replace batteries, inspect damage.
- Financial Literacy: earn, save, spend, give, trade, pitch, delayed reward.
- Time Awareness: now/next/later, morning/noon/night, timer, calendar.
- Digital Citizenship: private info, trusted people, ads, permissions, screen balance.
- Personal Safety: name, caregiver, safe adults, emergency basics, stay-with-adult rules.
- Travel Readiness: pack bag, destination plan, car safety, public behavior.
- Ownership And Responsibility: take care of belongings, return borrowed items, clean up after use.

Metrics:
- Completes routine step with fewer prompts.
- Identifies needed item for weather, meal, or activity.
- Uses tool safety rule.
- Saves for a delayed reward.
- States one private-information rule.

Lesson seeds:
- Build a morning checklist with photos.
- Sort groceries by food job: grow, go, repair, treat.
- Practice a safe screwdriver routine with a toy.
- Use tokens to plan a small project purchase.

## 4. Interpersonal & Social Dynamics

Purpose: Build connection, empathy, communication, boundaries, collaboration, negotiation, and group participation.

Subcategories:
- Empathy And Perspective: what someone wants, feels, sees, knows, or needs.
- Emotional Reading: faces, tone, posture, context clues.
- Conversation Skills: greeting, asking, answering, waiting, topic return.
- Listening And Reflecting: repeat back, check understanding, ask clarifying question.
- Conflict Resolution: stop, cool down, name issue, offer solution, repair.
- Negotiation: trade, timer, turn order, win-win, compromise.
- Boundaries And Consent: ask first, stop signal, private space, body autonomy.
- Sharing And Turn Taking: wait, pass, trade, choose timer, joint attention.
- Collaboration: roles, shared goal, check-in, handoff, team build.
- Leadership And Followership: lead a small task, follow another person's plan.
- Social Problem Solving: what happened, what choices exist, what might happen next.
- Community Participation: manners, helping, group norms, respect for shared spaces.

Metrics:
- Uses a boundary phrase or stop signal.
- Waits with a visual/timer support.
- Names another person's likely feeling or want.
- Completes a shared task with a role.
- Repairs after conflict with help.

Lesson seeds:
- Puppet conflict: two toys want the same block.
- Team build with roles: designer, holder, builder, checker.
- Practice "ask first" with household items.
- Use a timer to share a favorite tool or toy.

## 5. Physical Mastery & Environmental Stewardship

Purpose: Build body control, confidence, safety, sensory integration, fine-motor strength, outdoor competence, and care for the environment.

Subcategories:
- Gross Motor: run, jump, climb, crawl, balance, throw, catch.
- Proprioception: heavy work, pushing, pulling, carrying, body position awareness.
- Vestibular Balance: line walking, spinning limits, head/body orientation, obstacle courses.
- Fine Motor: pinch, grip, threading, cutting, tracing, stacking, tweezers.
- Bilateral Coordination: two-hand tasks, crossing midline, stabilizing with one hand.
- Hand-Eye Coordination: target games, catching, pouring, bead work, simple circuits.
- Tool Dexterity: child-safe tools, fasteners, clips, buttons, zippers, connectors.
- Health And Fitness: endurance, strength, flexibility, warm-up, cool-down.
- Environmental Stewardship: reduce, reuse, recycle, compost, repair, conserve.
- Nature Study: plants, animals, weather, soil, water, seasons.
- Situational Awareness: exits, safe spots, landmarks, listening, observation.
- Field Survival Basics: map, simple packing, weather, water, stay-put rule.

Metrics:
- Completes movement pattern with more control.
- Uses two hands for a coordinated task.
- Handles small materials with less frustration.
- Sorts resources correctly.
- Identifies safe place, exit, or landmark.

Lesson seeds:
- Tape-line balance mission before table work.
- Thread beads to prepare for small electronics work.
- Sort compost/recycle/trash after lunch.
- Draw a map of the home and mark safe spots.

## 6. Creative Expression & Intellectual Curiosity

Purpose: Build imagination, maker identity, art, music, design, questioning, wonder, iteration, and healthy failure recovery.

Subcategories:
- Visual Art: color, line, shape, texture, pattern, collage, sculpture.
- Music And Rhythm: beat, pattern, call/response, instruments, sound design.
- Storytelling And Pretend Play: characters, setting, problem, sequence, roleplay.
- Procedural Creativity: rule-based art, algorithms, pattern games, generative play.
- Maker Pipeline: idea, sketch, material choice, prototype, test, upgrade.
- Design Thinking: user need, constraint, choice, feedback, improvement.
- Curiosity And Questioning: why, what-if, how-could-we-find-out, wonder wall.
- Philosophical Inquiry: fairness, identity, personhood, nature, choices, future.
- Failure Recovery: oops, patch, retry, compare version, save lessons learned.
- Presentation And Sharing: show work, explain build, answer questions, receive feedback.
- Open Exploration: tinker table, loose parts, sensory bins, nature finds.
- Cross-Disciplinary Making: art plus math, music plus movement, story plus code.

Metrics:
- Starts with an idea and makes a version.
- Adds one improvement after feedback.
- Creates or follows a pattern/rule.
- Asks a question and tests or researches it.
- Shares what changed from V1 to V2.

Lesson seeds:
- Draw a plan, build it in blocks, add one upgrade.
- Make sticker patterns and translate them to claps.
- Ask "what if this tool had a new job?"
- Photograph V1 and V2 of a cardboard build.

## 7. Values, Ethics & Civic Duty

Purpose: Build integrity, fairness, responsibility, service, wise choice-making, identity, and contribution to family/community.

Subcategories:
- Moral Reasoning: fair/unfair, kind/unkind, harm/help, rule purpose.
- Golden And Platinum Rule: what I like vs what the other person needs.
- Integrity: truth, promise, action matches value, repair when promise breaks.
- Responsibility: jobs, belongings, family contribution, follow-through.
- Decision Architecture: choices, consequences, risk/reward, short/long term.
- Service And Contribution: help family, neighbor, community, shared spaces.
- Gratitude And Appreciation: notice help, say thanks, care for gifts.
- Stewardship: care for tools, toys, home, nature, time, attention.
- Courage And Advocacy: ask for help, speak up safely, protect boundaries.
- Civic Awareness: community helpers, rules, public spaces, voting as choice model.
- Calibration And Growth: version notes, not perfect, improve from evidence.
- Digital Ethics: honesty online, privacy, kindness, permissions, content trust.

Metrics:
- Keeps or repairs a small promise.
- Predicts one consequence of a choice.
- Identifies what another person wanted or needed.
- Completes a helper task.
- Names one improvement without shame.

Lesson seeds:
- Split snacks fairly, then ask if fair means same or enough.
- Choose one daily promise and check it at bedtime.
- Help clean a shared space and talk about community nodes.
- Compare V1 and V2 of a behavior: "What got better?"

## Missing Cross-Cutting Tags

These tags should be available on any lesson:
- Sensory demand: low, medium, high.
- Movement demand: seated, light movement, heavy work, outdoor.
- Language demand: nonverbal, low verbal, conversation, explanation.
- Social demand: solo, adult-paired, sibling/peer, group.
- Executive-function demand: easy start, multi-step, planning, shifting, waiting.
- Emotional demand: calm practice, mild frustration, recovery practice, conflict repair.
- Materials: none, household, printable, tool, device, outdoor, food.
- Evidence: observation, photo, score, drawing, build, audio note, student reflection.
- Student Edition status: not published, mission-ready, game-ready, completed, needs review.
